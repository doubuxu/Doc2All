Motivation

Dataset Bias: Dataset bias can result in models that are discriminatory or depend on spurious correlations. For instance, a face image restoration model may "hallucinate" features correlated with oversampled subgroups.

Parity and Diversity: The canonical approach is to categorize people using demographic labels. However, evaluating diversity by examining counts across subgroups fails to reflect the continuous nature of human phenotypic diversity.

Unknown Label Distributions: Most human-centric image datasets are web-scraped, lacking ground-truth information about the image subjects. Observational labels risk reifying stereotypes.

Contributions

Motivated by issues inherent to categorical labels, we aim to develop a tool that can evaluate the visual diversity of faces in unlabeled datasets.

Face Similarity (FAX) Dataset: We introduce a novel dataset, FAX, containing 638,180 odd-one-out face similarity judgments over 4,921 faces. Judgments correspond to the odd-one-out face in a triplet.

Model of Conditional Decision-Making: We introduce a model that learns to predict human judgments of face similarity conditioned on the annotator who generated the judgment.

Results

Interpretability: From learning on FAX, we note the materialization of distinct dimensions coinciding with commonly defined demographic subgroups.
