Langevin Autoencoders for Learning Deep Latent Variable Models

Motivation

Learning deep latent variable models

Latent variable models: p(x; θ) = ∫ p(x | z; θ)p(z)dz

Gradient over model parameters: ∇θ E_p_data(x)[log p(x; θ)] ≈ 1/n Σ ∇θ log p(x_i, z_i; θ)

  • To learn a latent variable model via gradient ascent, we need to approximate the expectation over the latent posterior p(z | x; θ).
  • Variational inference is a dominant approach but the approximation power is limited.
  • Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is capable of complex posteriors but is slow due to datapoint-wise iterations.

How can we amortize the cost of datapoint-wise iterations?

(a) Variational inference

(b) Langevin dynamics

Preliminary

Langevin dynamics

Langevin dynamics [2] is a gradient-based MCMC.

z' ~ q(z' | z) = N(z'; z + η∇z log p(x, z; θ), 2ηI)

By repeating this iterative update, the samples asymptotically approach the true posterior p(z | x; θ).

Method

Amortized Langevin dynamics

  • In our amortized Langevin dynamics (ALD), we prepare an encoder that maps the observed data into the latent variable, and run MCMC on its parameter space.
  • Markov chain on the latent space is implicitly performed by collecting the output of the encoder.
  • Our main theorem shows that the ALD is valid as MCMC if the encoder takes the form of: f_z|x(x; Φ) = Φg(x).

Langevin Autoencoder

ALD can be applied to the training of deep latent variable models (DLVMs) by slightly modifying the learning algorithm of traditional autoencoders.

We call this learning algorithm of DLVMs the Langevin autoencoder (LAE).

θ, Φ, ψ ← Initialize parameters

repeat

  x(1), ..., x(n) ~ p_data(x)

  for t = 0, ..., T-1 do

    V_t = -Σ log p(x_i, z_i; θ)

    Φ' ~ q(Φ' | Φ)

  end for

  θ ← θ - η∇θ Σ V_t

until convergence
