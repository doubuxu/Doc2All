A Simple 2D Navigation Problem

  • Task: Navigate to goal X; States: (x,y); Actions: unit length displacement
  • Forward process: Take random actions starting from goal
  • Reverse process: Train policy to reverse the forward trajectories

Effect of Time Conditioning

  • Analogous to the score function in diffusion, the Merlin policy is conditioned on time
  • This time indicates how "noisy" current state is, or how far we are from target
  • Policy takes optimal action far from goal and becomes uncertain as it approaches

The time conditioning affects policy uncertainty based on expected number of steps to reach the goal

Optimization Objective

Given an offline dataset D, we view trajectories as forward diffusion chains.

Behavior cloning maximizes the probability of reaching goal states:

θ* = arg max E [log πθ(a | s, g)]

Theorem: Given dataset D and goal distribution p(g), behavior cloning using a goal-conditioned policy maximizes a lower bound on the log-likelihood of goal states.

Constructing the Forward Process

  • Merlin: Use pre-collected offline trajectories, train policy using BC
  • Merlin-P: Learn reverse dynamics model to simulate trajectories backward
  • Merlin-NP: Use proposed trajectory stitching method

Experimental Results

Merlin outperforms previous methods, while being highly-efficient!

Conclusions and future work

  • Merlin constructs trajectories that "diffuse away" from goals and trains a policy to reverse them
  • Simple training dynamics indicate potential for scalability as shown by denoising diffusion models
  • Future work includes extending to online setting and partially observable environments
