Motivation

  • Matrices are ubiquitous -- can involve billions of elements making their storage and processing quite demanding.
  • Question: How to compress matrices via dimensionality reduction and quantization?
  • Several real-world matrices exhibit approximately low-rank structure due to inherent redundancy.

$A = \sum_{i=1}^{rank(A)} \sigma_i u_i v_i^T$

Leverage the approximate low-rank structure while quantizing matrices

Randomized Embeddings

$(1-\epsilon)\|z\|_2 \le \|Sz\|_2 \le (1+\epsilon)\|z\|_2 \quad \forall z \in V$

  • Subspace embeddings: Norm of vectors in the range space is approximately preserved with high probability.
  • Critical dimension: $m$ does not depend on original dimension $n$.

Random Embeddings (e.g., Gaussian) also help in quantization by reducing the dynamic range.

Our Algorithm: Low-Precision and Low Rank

Algorithm 1: LPLR: Randomized Low-Precision Low-Rank factorization
Input: Matrix A, sketch size m, Quantizers Q, Q'
1. Sample Gaussian sketching matrix S
2. Compute approximate basis by forming sketch AS
3. Quantize the approximate basis with Q to get Q(AS)
4. Find W* = arg min ||Q(AS)W - A||
5. Quantize W* using Q' to get Q'(W*)
6. Return L = Q(AS), R = Q'(W*)
  • We factorize $A \approx LR$, where entries of $L$ and $R$ are quantized with $B$ and $B'$ bits.
  • Compression ratio: $\frac{mnB + mdB'}{ndB_{nq}}$.
  • Low-precision computations are faster: Computing $Ax$ versus $L(Rx)$.
