Extending Kernel PCA through Dualization: Sparsity, Robustness and Fast Algorithms

Francesco Tonin, Alex Lambert, Panagiotis Patrinos, Johan Suykens

ESAT, KU Leuven, Belgium

Kernel PCA Problem

Given: n datapoints (xi) in X, feature map φ: X → H to a Hilbert space H.

Goal: find s directions in H that maximize the variance under orthonormal conditions. The KPCA optimization problem is

sup ||ΓW||²_F / 2

We use the following definitions:

  • The Stiefel manifold of orthonormal s-frames in H.
  • G(W) is the matrix such that G(W)ij = (wi, wj).
  • Γ: H^s → R^{n×s} is the linear operator s.t. for all (i, j).
  • G is the Gram matrix G = [k(xi, xj)], where k is the positive definite kernel function.

The usual way to solve (1) is through SVD of G, which is slow with larger n.

Paper TL;DR
We propose a duality framework to solve the KPCA problem faster, with extension to robust and sparse losses.

Difference of convex functions

Key idea: Rewrite (1) as a difference of convex functions

inf g(W) - f(ΓW)

Two key advantages:

  • Allows new gradient-based algorithm to solve KPCA efficiently without the SVD of G.
  • It becomes possible to slightly modify the loss function f to enforce specific properties such as robustness or sparsity.

Proposition 0.1 (Dual of difference of convex functions). Let U, K be two Hilbert spaces, g: U → R and f: K → R be two convex lower semi-continuous functions and Γ ∈ L(U, K).

Faster KPCA with Gradient Descent

Motivation for going from primal to dual: we show that g*(Γ^T H) is related to the nuclear norm of some low dimensional matrix.

Proposition 0.2. Let g be the indicator function of the Stiefel manifold and Γ as in Problem 1. Then for all H ∈ R^{n×s}:

g*(Γ^T H) = Tr √H^T G H

The computational complexity of computing the gradient of π:

  • Computation of H^T G H in O(sn²).
  • SVD of H^T G H in O(s³).

Beyond variance maximization

Typical loss function: square loss f = 1/2 ||·||²_F.

Problems: sensible to outliers, no sparsity.

Key idea: use a loss obtained with infimal convolution

f = 1/2 ||·||²_F □ Ψ

where Ψ is a well-chosen function that enforces robustness or sparsity.

DC Optimization

As f is a Moreau envelope, its gradient is always defined for all Y ∈ R^{n×s}.

∇(1/2 ||·||² □ Ψ)(Y) = Y - prox_Ψ(Y)

Robustness and sparsity

Denoting ||·||_* as the dual norm of ||·|| and the balls of radius t for these norms as B*_t and B_t, we extend KPCA with Huber and ε-insensitive objectives.

Extended Huber loss H_κ:

Ψ := κ ||·||, Ψ* = ι_{B*_κ}, prox_{Ψ*}(Y) = Proj_{B*_κ}(Y)

• Effect of κ for the losses on contaminated Iris dataset.

Extended ε-insensitive loss l_ε:

Ψ := ι_{B_ε}, Ψ* = ε ||·||_*, prox_{Ψ*}(Y) = Y - Proj_{B_ε}(Y)
