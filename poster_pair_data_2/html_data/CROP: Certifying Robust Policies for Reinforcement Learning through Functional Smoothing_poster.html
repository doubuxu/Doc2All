CROP: Certifying Robust Policies for Reinforcement Learning through Functional Smoothing

Fan Wu, Linyi Li, Zijian Huang, Yevgeniy Vorobeychik, Ding Zhao, Bo Li

University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign
Washington University in St. Louis
Carnegie Mellon University
github.com/lucas-fan/CROP

Background & Motivation

  • Testing and improving the robustness of safety-critical applications before their massive deployment to real-world is of great importance.
  • Robustness certification for a given learning algorithm is needed to end the repeated game between the attackers and defenders.

Threat Model

Testing-time evasion attack on agent's observation. Adversary can apply l2-bounded perturbations.

Certification Goal

Provide test-time certification for the performance of the trained agent in the perturbed environment.

  • 1. Providing criteria for robustness certification.
  • 2. Proposing certification strategies corresponding to different certification criteria.

Certification Criteria

Criterion 1 (state-level): Per-state action stability. Maximum Perturbation Magnitude ε.

Criterion 2 (trajectory-level): Cumulative reward lower bound. Lower bound of perturbed cumulative reward J.

CROP Framework

Per-State Action Certification (CROP-LoAct): Local randomized smoothing on states. Action-value functional smoothing.

  • Lipschitz continuity of Q w.r.t. the state input.
  • Lower bound of ε calculation based on smoothed policy.

Cumulative Reward Certification

CROP-GRe: Global smoothing on trajectories.

  • Expectation bound via mean smoothing.
  • Percentile bound via percentile smoothing.

CROP-LoRe: Local smoothing + adaptive search

Sequence of certified radii for actions and adaptive search algorithm.

1. Trajectory exploration and expansion: obtain the lower bound for ε (DFS).

2. Perturbation magnitude growth: obtain a new ε (priority queue).

Experiments

Setup: Four RL environments: Freeway, Pong, CartPole, Highway. Nine DQN variants including StdTrain, AdvTrain, etc.

Robustness Certification for Per-State Action

  • GradDQN and RadialRL achieve the highest certified radius on Freeway.
  • Periodic patterns can be observed in Pong.

Robustness Certification for Cumulative Reward

  • Under global smoothing, percentile bound is tighter than expectation bound.
  • The certification is relatively tight compared with the empirical result.
