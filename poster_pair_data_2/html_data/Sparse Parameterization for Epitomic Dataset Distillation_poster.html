Dataset distillation

Dataset distillation aims to obtain smaller synthetic datasets that capture the essential information of the originals.

The success of dataset distillation relies on two essential factors:

  • Proper parameterization of the synthetic dataset
  • Effective design of the matching objective to align it with the original

Previous explorations

Spatial redundancy exists within individual synthetic images and between different synthetic images.

  • Naive image-independent parameterization: each learnable parameter basis (synthetic image) is optimized independently
  • Recent efficient parameterization: either only consider the connections between synthetic images, or do not reduce the redundancy inside the synthetic image thoroughly

Sparse parameterization

Our sparse parameterization strategy leverages principles from representation learning paradigms, including convolutional neural network, vision transformer, dictionary learning, and sparse coding.

  • Spatial-agnostic epitomic tokens (SAETs) E are shared among all patches in all synthetic images, reducing spatial redundancy
  • Sparse coding matrices (SCMs) Ai are stored in a sparse manner, eliminating inefficient parameters and bringing better generalization
  • Feature-recurrent network (FReeNet) Φθ recurrently use the sparsely combined features, alleviating storage burden
