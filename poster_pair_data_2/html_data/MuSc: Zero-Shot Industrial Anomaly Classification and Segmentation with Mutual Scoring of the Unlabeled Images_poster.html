MuSc: Zero-Shot Industrial Anomaly Classification and Segmentation with Mutual Scoring of the Unlabeled Images

Xurui Li1,*, Ziming Huang1,*, Feng Xue3, Yu Zhou1,2

1Huazhong University of Science and Technology, 2Artificial Intelligence Research Institute, Wuhan JingCe Electronic Group Co.,LTD, 3University of Trento
github.com/hustvl/MuSc

Motivation

A rich amount of normal information implicit in unlabeled test images can be exploited for anomaly detection.

A discriminative characteristic: the normal image patches could find a relatively large number of similar patches in other unlabeled images, while the abnormal ones only have a few similar patches.

Contributions

  • The first method that only uses the unlabeled test images for industrial anomaly detection.
  • We reveal the potential capability of normal and abnormal patches contained in unlabeled images.
  • 21.1% PRO gains and 21.9% seg-AP gains on MVTec AD and 19.4% seg-AP gains on VisA.

Framework

Mutual Scoring Mechanism

Image Ij assigns a score to each aggregated patch token of image Ii

ai,lm,r(Ij) = minn || p̂i,lm,r - p̂j,ln,r ||2

Interval Average (minimum 30%)

āi,lm,r = (1/K) Σk∈[1,K] ai,lm,r(Ik)

Mean: r and l

aim = (1/L) Σl∈{1,...,L} (1/3) Σr∈{1,3,5} āi,lm,r

RsCIN

Image-level feature Fi extracted by ViT. Similarity matrix Wi,j = Fi · Fj

Multi-window Mask Mk(i,j)

Re-Scoring D(i,i) = Σ Mk ⊙ W(i,j)

Universal module for other AD methods

LNAMD

  • Neighborhood range: 1x1, 3x3, 5x5
  • Aggregate function: Adaptive average pooling
  • Multi-degree aggregation: To segment abnormal regions of varying sizes.
